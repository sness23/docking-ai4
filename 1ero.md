All ligand atoms were refined at partial occupancy (0.25–0.50) with a single alternate conformation (altloc A); therefore the intermolecular distances reported below should be interpreted with caution.  

The boronic acid moiety of compound 1 was covalently linked to the catalytic Ser70, with the boron atom (BJP300 B) forming a tetrahedral adduct to the Ser70 Oγ at 1.46 Å.  The resulting boronate oxyanion (OB1) was positioned in the canonical oxyanion hole: backbone amide nitrogens of Ser70 and Ala237 approached OB1 at 2.85 Å and 3.42 Å, respectively.

A dense hydrogen-bonding network surrounded the boronate.  The Ser70 side chain donated hydrogen bonds from Oγ to both OB1 (2.41 Å) and OB2 (2.44 Å), while the Ser70 backbone N approached OB2 at 2.93 Å.  Downstream in the β-lactamase active site, the Ala237 carbonyl accepted hydrogen bonds from OB1 (3.03 Å) and the ligand amide N1 (3.49 Å), and its backbone N contacted OB1 (3.42 Å).  The adjacent Ser235 provided three contacts: its backbone carbonyl accepted from O3 (2.68 Å), and its side-chain Oγ engaged O3 (2.29 Å) and O4 (3.03 Å).  Gly236 contributed a backbone N-to-O3 hydrogen bond (3.27 Å).  On the distal aryl carboxylate, Arg243 donated a side-chain NH1 hydrogen bond to O4 (3.12 Å).  The Asn132 side chain formed a bifurcated interaction with the ligand phenolic oxygen O6: ND2 donated at 2.54 Å and OD1 accepted at 3.48 Å.  Asn170 acted similarly at the boronate: OD1 accepted from OB1 (3.35 Å) and donated to OB2 (2.56 Å), while ND2 approached OB2 at 3.44 Å.  Finally, Glu166 engaged the boronate through both carboxylate oxygens (OE1–OB2, 2.57 Å; OE2–OB2, 3.37 Å).  Several of these polar contacts satisfied formal charges to create salt bridges: OB2 with Glu166 OE1/OE2, O3 with Lys234 NZ (3.54 Å), and O4 with Arg243 NH1/NH2 (3.12/3.96 Å).

The ligand’s two aromatic rings were buried in a predominantly hydrophobic pocket lined by Ser70, Ser130, Tyr105, Val216, Asn132, Asn170, Glu166, Ser235, Gly236, and Ala237; closest heavy-atom approaches ranged from 3.38 Å (Ser130) to about 4.5 Å.  An offset π–π stacking interaction was observed between the ligand phenyl ring and Tyr105 at 5.27 Å.  No ordered water molecules were detected within 6 Å of the ligand, indicating complete displacement of the conserved active-site waters in this crystal form.  Several protein–protein hydrogen bonds that flank the ligand were retained: Ser70 Oγ hydrogen-bonded to Lys73 NZ (2.9 Å), Glu166 OE1 hydrogen-bonded to Asn132 OD1 (3.31 Å) and to Lys73 NZ (3.39 Å), and the Ser235–Gly236 peptide bond remained closed (O–N, 2.22 Å).  These intramolecular contacts, lying 1.5–4 Å from the ligand, defined the pre-organized catalytic architecture into which compound 1 was accommodated.
