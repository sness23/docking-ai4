A covalent bond was observed between the catalytic Ser70 and compound 1.  The Oγ atom of Ser70 was linked to the ligand boron atom (BJP300 B) at a distance of 1.46 Å, yielding the expected tetrahedral boronate adduct within the active site.

The tetrahedral oxyanion was accommodated by the classical oxyanion hole.  The backbone amide nitrogen of Ser70 donated a hydrogen bond to the boronate oxygen OB1 (2.85 Å), and the corresponding nitrogen of Ala237 approached the same oxygen at 3.42 Å.  Additional main-chain contacts further anchored the ligand: Ser70 N was hydrogen-bonded to the second boronate oxygen OB2 (2.93 Å); the backbone nitrogen of Gly236 interacted with the ligand carboxylate oxygen O3 (3.27 Å); and the Ala237 carbonyl oxygen formed hydrogen bonds with OB1 (3.03 Å) and with the ligand amide nitrogen N1 (3.49 Å).  A close contact (2.68 Å) was also present between the Ser235 backbone carbonyl oxygen and ligand oxygen O3.  The Ser235 side-chain hydroxyl contributed two hydrogen bonds, one to O3 (2.29 Å) and a second to O4 (3.03 Å).

Several side chains supplied additional polar interactions.  The Ser70 Oγ, although covalently attached to boron, lay 2.41 Å from OB1 and 2.44 Å from OB2, completing the tetrahedral coordination sphere.  Asn170 formed a triad of hydrogen bonds: OD1 contacted OB1 (3.35 Å) and OB2 (2.56 Å), while ND2 approached OB2 (3.44 Å).  The ligand carboxylate was further recognized by Arg243, whose NH1 donated a hydrogen bond to O4 (3.12 Å), and by Lys234, whose NZ formed a salt bridge with O3 (3.54 Å).  At the distal end of the molecule, Asn132 engaged the phenyl-carboxylate oxygen O6 through both OD1 (3.48 Å) and ND2 (2.54 Å).  The boronate oxygen OB2 also accepted hydrogen bonds from Glu166: OE1 lay 2.57 Å away and OE2 3.37 Å, establishing a bifurcated electrostatic contact that can be described as a short salt bridge.

Compound 1 was enclosed by a predominantly hydrophobic shell comprising Ser70, Ser130, Tyr105, Val216, Asn132, Glu166, Asn170, Gly236, Ser235, and Ala237, with closest heavy-atom approaches ranging from 3.38 Å (Ser130) to ~4.5 Å.  The phenyl ring of the ligand stacked against the aromatic face of Tyr105, the centroids lying 5.08 Å apart, consistent with a π-stacking interaction.  No ordered water molecules bridged the protein–ligand interface in the present structure.
