No covalent linkage was observed between the ligand and the enzyme in the crystal structure, and no interactions corresponding to an oxyanion hole were identified. All ligand interactions were mediated by noncovalent contacts.

Direct hydrogen bonding was dominated by side‐chain donors and acceptors. The carboxylate oxygens of OAb3 engaged the guanidinium groups of multiple arginine residues: OAb3‐F O72 to Arg224/A NH2 (2.61 Å), OAb3‐G O72 to Arg224/B NH2 (2.70 Å) and OAb3‐H O72 to Arg224/C NH2 (2.90 Å); OAb3‐G O71 to Gln266/B OE1 (2.94 Å) and OAb3‐H O71 to Gln266/C NE2 (2.92 Å); OAb3‐I O23 to Arg292/C NH2 (3.29 Å); OAb3‐I O41 to Arg338/A NH2 (3.29 Å) and OAb3‐J O41 to Arg338/C NH2 (2.53 Å) and NH1 (3.43 Å); OAb3‐I O33 to Asp339/A OD2 (2.38 Å) and OAb3‐J O33 to Asp339/C OD2 (2.81 Å). Electrostatic (salt‐bridge) interactions paralleled the hydrogen bonds. The guanidinium groups of Arg224 in all three chains and Arg221/B and Arg221/C each formed salt bridges to OAb3 carboxylates (e.g., Arg224/A NH2–OAb3‐F O72 at 2.61 Å, Arg221/B NH2–OAb3‐G O71 at 3.43 Å). The carboxylate of OAA1‐F O11 formed salt bridges with both NH1 and NH2 of Arg374/A (3.25–3.36 Å), and OAA1‐F O31/O41 engaged Glu378/A OE1 and OE2 (2.47–3.29 Å). Analogous salt bridges were observed with Arg374/B and Arg374/C to OAA1‐G/H O11, with Glu378/B and Glu378/C to OAA1‐G/H O31/O41, with Arg292 and Arg338 to OAb3‐I O23/O41, with Asp339 to OAb3‐I/J O33, with Arg394 to OAA1‐I/J O32, and with Lys447 to OAA1‐I/J O72, with distances ranging from 2.25 to 3.96 Å.

The ligand was further encompassed by a hydrophobic shell composed of residues such as Ala264, Ile323, Met320, Leu356, Val261, Val381, and Thr287/328/390/415, which made van der Waals contacts with closest approaches of 3.50–4.45 Å. Aromatic side chains of Trp219, Trp226, Tyr233 and Phe346 were positioned at edge‐to‐face or parallel orientations relative to the ligand scaffold, with centroid‐to‐centroid distances of 4.81–5.45 Å consistent with pi‐stacking or pi–van der Waals interactions. No ordered water molecules were observed mediating ligand–protein hydrogen bonds in the binding site.
