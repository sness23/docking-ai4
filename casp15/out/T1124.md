No covalent linkage was observed between SAH and the protein, and no oxyanion hole was formed around the ligand in the crystal structure. Instead, SAH binding was stabilized by an extensive network of direct hydrogen bonds and electrostatic interactions. The adenine moiety of SAH engaged the protein main chain by forming hydrogen bonds from the ligand N atom to the backbone carbonyl oxygen of Gly202 (2.84 Å) and Ala268 (2.85 Å), and from the ligand N1 atom to the backbone amide nitrogen of Phe253 (3.00 Å). The ligand N6 atom donated a hydrogen bond to the side chain OD1 of Asp252 (2.96 Å), and the ligand N1 atom was positioned within salt‐bridge distance of the same carboxylate (Asp252 OD1 at 3.85 Å).  

The ribose and homocysteine portions of SAH were further anchored by multiple side‐chain interactions. The ribose O3′ oxygen accepted hydrogen bonds from Asp225 OD2 (2.67 Å) and, more weakly, from Asp225 OD1 (3.39 Å), while the ribose O2′ oxygen was hydrogen‐bonded to Asp225 OD1 (2.67 Å). The nonbridging O4′ atom of the ligand was situated at salt‐bridge distance from Asp225 OD2 (3.91 Å). The terminal hydroxyl (OXT) of the homocysteine tail formed hydrogen bonds to the backbone carbonyl of Ala268 (3.19 Å) and to the side chain ND2 of Asn269 (2.82 Å), placing both residues within van der Waals contact distances (3.72–3.87 Å).

A hydrophobic shell surrounded the bound SAH, comprising residues Ile170, Val167, Ile270, Leu273, Tyr163, Tyr208, Val226, Gly202, Gly204, Gly251, Cys203 and Phe253 from each protomer. These residues made multiple van der Waals contacts with SAH at distances ranging from approximately 3.2 Å (Phe253) to 4.5 Å. The adenine ring of SAH was additionally sandwiched in a parallel π‐stacking arrangement with the phenyl ring of Phe253, at 4.03 Å in chain A and 4.32 Å in chain B. No water‐mediated hydrogen bonds were detected in the ligand‐binding site.
