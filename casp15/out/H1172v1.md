No covalent linkage was observed between AGS and the protein. The oxyanion hole was defined by two backbone amide nitrogens coordinating the ligand’s anionic oxygen. In subunit A, the O2A atom of AGS600/I was positioned within hydrogen‐bonding distance of the Thr51/A N (3.49 Å) and Thr52/A N (3.13 Å). In subunit C, the corresponding O1B atom of AGS600/K was located 3.45 Å from the Gly49/C N and 2.91 Å from the Lys50/C N. These interactions are consistent with stabilization of a tetrahedral adduct in the canonical oxyanion hole.

A network of direct hydrogen bonds was observed between AGS and side‐chain donors in all three subunits. In subunit A, the guanidinium nitrogens of Arg156/A (NH1, NH2) donated to O3G at 3.20 Å and 3.48 Å, respectively, while Arg203/A NH2 contacted O3B (3.45 Å). The guanidinium group of Arg6/A formed hydrogen bonds to O1A (NH1, NH2 at 3.07 Å and 3.35 Å) and to O2A (NH1 at 3.50 Å). Equivalent interactions were observed in subunits B and C, with Arg156, Arg203 and Arg6 donating hydrogen bonds to AGS oxygens at 3.14–3.46 Å. The adenine N6 atom accepted hydrogen bonds from the backbone carbonyl of Ile14 in each subunit (2.92–3.05 Å) and from the Tyr166 hydroxyl (3.14–3.16 Å). Main‐chain interactions included the backbone N of Gly47 coordinating O3B (3.09–3.24 Å) and the backbone N of Leu5 to O2′ in subunits B (2.81 Å) and C (3.11 Å).

Additional hydrogen bonds were mediated by hydroxyl side chains of threonine and the ε‐amino group of lysine. In subunit A, Thr51/A OG1 donated to O2B (2.84 Å) and Thr52/A OG1 to O2A (2.85 Å), with analogous contacts in subunits B and C (Thr51 OG1–O2B at 3.02–3.04 Å; Thr52 OG1–O1A at 2.80–3.06 Å). Lys50 provided both backbone and side‐chain contacts: its backbone N hydrogen bonded to O1B of AGS600 at 2.91–3.08 Å, and its NZ atom formed hydrogen bonds to the S1G atom of AGS600 at 2.79–2.86 Å.

Electrostatic interactions complemented the hydrogen‐bonding network. The phosphate oxygens of AGS600/I engaged in salt bridges with Arg6/A NH1/NH2 (3.07–3.92 Å) and with Lys50/A NZ (3.15 Å), while AGS600/J and AGS600/K phosphates formed analogous salt bridges with Arg6, Arg203 and Lys50 in subunits B and C at 3.14–3.96 Å. In each monomer, one phosphate oxygen also interacted with Glu113 OE2 (3.74–3.84 Å). A hydrophobic shell surrounded the ligand, involving Pro202, Pro7, Gly49, Leu48, Ile174 and Tyr13, with closest‐approach distances ranging from 3.47 Å to 4.46 Å. No aromatic π‐stacking or water‐mediated hydrogen bonds were observed.
