No covalent linkage between NAG and the protein was observed in the structure. The oxyanion hole was formed by the backbone amide nitrogens of Ala41 and Trp42, which coordinated the ring O7 atom of the second N-acetylglucosamine (NAG2). In protomer A, the Ala41 N–NAG2 O7 and Trp42 N–NAG2 O7 distances were 2.88 Å and 2.93 Å, respectively; in protomer B, the corresponding Ala41 N–O7 and Trp42 N–O7 distances were 3.19 Å and 3.16 Å.

In protomer A, the side-chain OD2 of Asp149 formed hydrogen bonds to NAG1 O3 (3.25 Å) and NAG2 O5 (3.29 Å), while both OD1 and OD2 contacted NAG2 O6 at 2.86 Å. The indole NE1 of Trp16 hydrogen-bonded to NAG2 O3 at 2.68 Å in protomer A, and in protomer B to NAG2 O3 at 2.98 Å and to NAG2 N2 at 3.40 Å.

Estimated centroid-centroid distances ranged from 5.03 Å to 5.46 Å, consistent with pi–stacking or van der Waals contacts. No water-mediated bridges were observed.
