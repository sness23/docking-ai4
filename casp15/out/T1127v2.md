No covalent bond was observed between coenzyme A (COA) and the protein. In both independent ligand sites, the pyrophosphate oxygen O1A was stabilized in an oxyanion hole formed by backbone amide nitrogens. In chain A, O1A was coordinated by the main‐chain N atoms of Lys145 (3.15 Å), Arg144 (3.23 Å) and Gly146 (2.86 Å). In the symmetry‐related chain B, O1A was hydrogen‐bonded to the backbone N atoms of Gly146 (2.75 Å) and Lys145 (3.17 Å).

A network of direct hydrogen bonds extended from the phosphate and pantetheine moieties to both side‐chain and backbone donors. In chain A, the guanidinium NE and NH1 atoms of Arg144 formed hydrogen bonds to O7A (3.39 Å) and to O1A (3.23 Å) of COA, while the backbone N of Arg144 also contacted O5A (2.99 Å). The side chain of Arg143 in chain B donated a hydrogen bond from NE to the nitrogen N8P of the 3′‐phosphate (3.46 Å). The polar side chains of Asn175 (ND2–O5P, 2.78 Å) and Asn177 (ND2–OAP, 3.22 Å; ND2–O5P, 3.02 Å) in chain A engaged the pyrophosphate. Asn180/A formed multiple contacts to the same pyrophosphate: ND2–O7A (3.44 Å), ND2–O9A (2.49 Å), OD1–O9A (3.36 Å), and its backbone carbonyl oxygen accepted a hydrogen bond from O9A (3.00 Å). The adenine ribose was further anchored by Gln184/A NE2 to O2B (2.69 Å) and O3B (3.23 Å), and by Gln184/A OE1 to O7A (3.45 Å) and O9A (2.41 Å). Main‐chain amides of Gly148 (to O4A, 2.90 Å in chain A; to O5A, 2.76 Å in chain B), Lys149 (to O2A, 2.87 Å in chain A), Met138 (chain A N–O9P, 2.93 Å; O–O9P, 3.42 Å), and Phe181 (N–O9A, 3.48 Å) also contributed to ligand binding. A hydrogen bond from Lys149/B NZ to O9A (3.35 Å) completed the network in chain B.

Several electrostatic interactions consistent with salt bridges were observed between the negatively charged phosphate oxygens and positively charged side chains. In chain A, the O7A atom of COA400 formed a salt bridge with Arg144 NH1 (3.39 Å), while OAP and O9P contacted Arg143 NE at 3.92 Å and 3.94 Å, respectively, and N4P engaged Arg143 NH1 (3.70 Å). In chain B, the 3′‐phosphate oxygens O8A and O9A interacted with Lys149 NZ at 3.63 Å and 3.35 Å, and the N8P atom formed an electrostatic contact with Arg143 NE (3.46 Å).

The hydrophobic and van der Waals contacts formed a shell around the ligand. Residues with closest‐approach distances below 4.5 Å included Ile136, Phe31, Phe137, Phe181, Ala178, Met138, Arg143, Arg144, Lys145, Gly146, Gly148, Lys149, Asn175, Asn177 and Gln184. These side‐chain and main‐chain atoms collectively packed against the pantetheine and adenine moieties, with the closest contacts observed for Arg143/B (3.49 Å), Arg144/B (3.66 Å), Phe31/A (3.61 Å) and Phe181/A (3.87 Å). No aromatic π–π stacking or water‐mediated bridges were detected.
