No covalent linkage was observed between ADP and H1172v3_lig. The oxyanion hole was formed by the backbone amide nitrogens of Gly47, Leu48, Gly49 and Lys50, which coordinated the β-phosphate O1B atom of ADP. In subunit A, the distances from O1B to the N atoms of Gly47, Leu48, Gly49 and Lys50 were 3.34 Å, 3.30 Å, 3.22 Å and 3.19 Å, respectively. Equivalent interactions were observed in subunits D, E and F with O1B distances of 2.98–3.39 Å to the corresponding main-chain nitrogens.

Multiple direct hydrogen bonds were formed between ADP and protein side chains or backbone atoms. The β-phosphate O2B atom donated hydrogen bonds to the backbone N (2.91 Å) and side-chain OG1 (2.64 Å) of Thr51 in subunit A, and analogous Thr51 contacts (2.34–3.43 Å) were seen in the other subunits. The α-phosphate O1A atom formed a hydrogen bond to the side-chain OG1 of Thr52 (3.08 Å) in subunit A. The N6 atom of the adenine ring formed a hydrogen bond to the backbone carbonyl O of Ile14 (2.92 Å) and to the phenolic OH of Tyr166 (3.30 Å) in subunit A; equivalent adenine contacts (3.26–3.32 Å) were found in subunits D and E. Additional hydrogen bonding to phosphate oxygens was provided by the guanidinium NH2 of Arg203 (O2B···NH2, 2.89 Å) and by the NH1 of Arg6 (O2A···NH1, 3.42 Å) in subunit A.

Electrostatic interactions were observed between the negatively charged phosphate and ribose oxygens of ADP and multiple positively charged side chains. In subunit A, a salt bridge was formed between O5′ of the ribose and NH2 of Arg203 (3.59 Å), and between O1B and NZ of Lys50 (2.75 Å). The O1A atom engaged NH1 of Arg6 at 3.80 Å, while O2A and O3B made further contacts with the NH1/NH2 of Arg6 and the NH2 of Arg203 in the range of 2.89–3.93 Å. Comparable salt-bridge geometries were present in subunits D and F.

The ADP molecule was surrounded by a hydrophobic shell composed of Pro202, Pro7, Ile174, Leu5, Leu48 and the backbone methylenes of Gly47 and Gly49. Closest van der Waals approach distances ranged from 3.55 Å (Pro7) to 4.45 Å (Tyr13 in one subunit), with multiple contacts clustering around Pro202 (3.75–4.13 Å) and Gly49 (3.91–4.28 Å). No aromatic π-stacking interactions or water-mediated hydrogen-bond bridges were observed in the ADP binding site.
