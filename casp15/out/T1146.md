Several direct hydrogen bonds were formed between NAG401 and active‐site residues. The side‐chain guanidinium NH1 of Arg132 donated a hydrogen bond to the O4 atom of NAG401 (2.73 Å). The side‐chain carboxylate OD2 of Asp93 accepted a hydrogen bond from the O6 atom of NAG401 (2.38 Å). The backbone carbonyl O atom of His134 accepted a hydrogen bond from the N2 atom of NAG401 (2.88 Å), and the side‐chain ND1 of His134 formed a hydrogen bond with the O3 atom of NAG401 (3.02 Å).

Electrostatic interactions were observed between NAG401 and charged side chains. The O4 atom of NAG401 formed salt bridges with both NH1 (2.73 Å) and NE (3.95 Å) of Arg132. The O3 atom of NAG401 interacted with the positively charged ND1 of His134 at 3.02 Å. The O6 atom of NAG401 formed salt bridges with both OD2 (2.38 Å) and OD1 (3.57 Å) of Asp93, and the O5 atom of the ligand contacted OD2 of Asp93 at 3.88 Å.

A hydrophobic shell surrounded NAG401, comprising side chains of Asp93 (closest approach 3.87 Å), His134 (4.44 Å), Leu251 (4.47 Å), Phe135 (4.38 Å), Thr95 (4.17 Å), and Thr96 (3.68 Å), which collectively engaged van der Waals contacts with the ligand. Aromatic stacking interactions were suggested between the sugar ring of NAG401 and the imidazole of His134 (4.87 Å) as well as the phenyl ring of Phe135 (5.36 Å). No water‐mediated bridges were detected.
