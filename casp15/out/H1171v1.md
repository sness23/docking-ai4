No covalent bond was observed between AGS and the protein. The phosphonate O1A and O1B atoms were positioned in the oxyanion hole and coordinated by backbone amide nitrogens. In protomer A, O1A accepted hydrogen bonds from the backbone nitrogens of Thr52/A (3.10 Å) and Thr51/A (3.46 Å), while O1B received hydrogen bonds from the backbone nitrogens of Lys50/A (2.81 Å) and Gly49/A (3.48 Å). Equivalent oxyanion‐hole interactions were observed in protomers B, C, and D with distances of 3.06–3.46 Å for the Thr51/Thr52 pair and 3.39–3.46 Å for the Lys50/Gly49 pair.

A network of direct hydrogen bonds anchored the adenine, ribose and phosphonate moieties of AGS. The adenine N6 atom accepted a hydrogen bond from the backbone amide of Ile14 (2.89–2.94 Å across protomers) and from the hydroxyl of Tyr166 side chain (3.02–3.48 Å); in protomers B–D the adenine N7 atom also accepted a hydrogen bond from Tyr166 OH (3.18–3.40 Å). The ribose O2′ atom accepted a hydrogen bond from the backbone carbonyl of Leu5 (3.02–3.24 Å), and the O3′ atom accepted a hydrogen bond from the side chain NH1 of Arg6 (3.29–3.34 Å). The phosphonate S1G atom accepted hydrogen bonds from Lys50 NZ (2.70–2.82 Å) and Thr144 OG1 (3.20–3.47 Å). One phosphonate oxygen (O2B) accepted a hydrogen bond from Thr51 OG1 (2.33–3.02 Å), while the other oxygens (O2A, O3B, O3G) formed hydrogen bonds with side chains of Arg6 (NH1, 3.22–3.69 Å), Arg203 (NH1/NH2, 2.57–3.46 Å) and Arg156 (NH1/NH2, 2.57–3.92 Å).

Electrostatic interactions supplemented these hydrogen bonds. Salt bridges were observed between phosphonate oxygens and positively charged residues: O1A and O2A with Arg6 NH1 (3.34–3.55 Å), O1B with Lys50 NZ (3.15–3.36 Å), and O2A with Arg203 NH2 (3.46–3.74 Å). Conversely, O2A engaged negatively charged Glu113 OE1/OE2 at 3.68–3.87 Å in a symmetry‐related protomer. A hydrophobic shell of aliphatic and aromatic residues—including the methylene groups of Arg6, Arg203, Ile174, Leu48, Leu5, Pro202, Pro7 and the phenolic ring of Tyr166—was observed at closest‐approach distances of 3.59–4.49 Å. An offset π‐stacking interaction between the phenyl ring of Tyr13 and the AGS adenine ring was observed with a ring‐centroid distance of 5.37 Å. No water‐mediated hydrogen bonds were detected.
