No covalent linkage was observed between ADP and the protein. The β-​phosphate O1B atom of each symmetry‐related ADP (molecules L, M, and N) was coordinated by the backbone amide nitrogens of Gly47, Leu48, Gly49, and Lys50 at distances of 3.29 Å, 3.06 Å, 2.98 Å, and 3.28 Å for ADP600/L, 3.02 Å, 2.84 Å, 2.73 Å, and 3.13 Å for ADP600/M, and 2.86 Å, 3.17 Å, and 3.44 Å for ADP600/N. The O2B atom of the β-phosphate was further stabilized by backbone nitrogens of Thr51 (2.96 Å, 2.89 Å, and 2.65 Å for L, M, and N, respectively) and of Lys50 (3.33 Å, 3.41 Å, and 3.43 Å). The O3B atom of ADP600/N was also positioned within hydrogen‐bonding distance to the backbone nitrogens of Gly47 (2.86 Å), Leu48 (3.17 Å), and Gly49 (3.44 Å), thereby completing the oxyanion hole.

Multiple direct hydrogen bonds were observed between ADP and side chains in the phosphate‐ribose region. The NH2 groups of Arg203 in two independent protomers (chains E and F) formed hydrogen bonds to O5′ of ADP600/M (2.65 Å) and ADP600/N (3.03 Å), respectively. The NZ atom of Lys50 in chain F also donated a hydrogen bond to O3B of ADP600/N at 2.84 Å. In addition, the OG1 hydroxyl of Thr51 engaged O2B of the β-phosphate in each copy (2.68 Å for L, 2.71 Å for M) and also contacted O1B in ADP600/N (2.39 Å). The OG1 of Thr52 formed a hydrogen bond to O1A of the α-phosphate in ADP600/M (3.03 Å) and in ADP600/N (2.81 Å).

Hydrogen bonds to the adenine base were mediated by backbone and side‐chain atoms in the nucleotide‐binding pocket. The carbonyl oxygen of Ile14 accepted hydrogen bonds from N6 of adenine in ADP600/L (3.40 Å), ADP600/M (3.14 Å), and ADP600/N (3.05 Å). The phenolic OH of Tyr166 in chain D donated hydrogen bonds to N6 and N7 of ADP600/L at distances of 3.21 Å and 3.37 Å; analogous interactions were observed with N6 of ADP600/M (3.29 Å) and ADP600/N (3.49 Å).

Electrostatic interactions consistent with salt bridges were observed between positively charged side chains and phosphate oxygens. In chain E, the NE atom of Arg203 was within 3.89 Å of O5′ of ADP600/M, and its NH2 engaged both O5′ (2.65 Å) and O2A (3.68 Å). In chain F, the NH2 of Arg203 contacted O5′ (3.03 Å) and O2A (3.62 Å) of ADP600/N, while Lys50 NZ formed a salt bridge to O3B (2.84 Å) of ADP600/N.

A hydrophobic shell surrounded the bound nucleotide, comprising side chains and backbone atoms from residues Arg203, Arg6, Gly49, Ile174, Leu48, Leu5, Pro202, Pro7, and Tyr166. Closest‐approach distances ranged from 3.54 Å (Pro202/E) to 4.49 Å (Leu48/F, Tyr166/D), indicating van der Waals contacts that encapsulated the adenine and ribose moieties without aromatic π‐stacking or water‐mediated bridges.
