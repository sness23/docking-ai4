No covalent bond was observed between NAG and the protein. The oxyanion hole was formed by the backbone amide nitrogens of TRP11/B and MET10/B, which coordinated the O7 atom of the second N-acetylglucosamine (NAG2/D) at distances of 2.78 Å and 3.31 Å, respectively. No salt bridges or water-mediated hydrogen bonds were observed in the binding site.

Multiple direct hydrogen bonds were formed to the first sugar unit (NAG1/D). The O3 atom of NAG1/D accepted a hydrogen bond from the backbone carbonyl oxygen of ASN35/A (3.39 Å), and the N2 atom of NAG1/D donated a hydrogen bond to the side chain OD1 of ASN35/A (2.79 Å). The O7 atom of NAG1/D adopted hydrogen bonds with the side chain OG1 of THR9/A (3.47 Å) and the backbone amide nitrogen of TRP11/A (3.03 Å). The second sugar ring (NAG2/D) was further stabilized by hydrogen bonds from its O5 and O6 atoms to the backbone carbonyl oxygen of ASN35/A (3.49 Å and 2.84 Å, respectively), and by bifurcated hydrogen bonds from O6 to both the backbone amide nitrogen and carbonyl oxygen of ILE37/A (2.98 Å and 3.33 Å, respectively). In addition, the O3 atom of NAG2/D accepted a hydrogen bond from the backbone carbonyl oxygen of ASN35/B (2.77 Å), and the N2 atom of NAG2/D donated a hydrogen bond to the side chain OD1 of ASN35/B (2.93 Å).
