Compound 2 was covalently linked to the enzyme through the canonical tetrahedral boronate adduct.  The boron atom of the inhibitor (BJH300 B) was bonded to the Oγ of Ser70 at 1.45 Å, placing the ligand in the acyl-enzyme site and anchoring it for the ensuing network of contacts.  The resulting tetrahedral intermediate was stabilized by the classical TEM-1 oxyanion hole: the boronate oxygen OB1 accepted hydrogen bonds from the backbone amide nitrogens of Ser70 (2.80 Å) and Ala237 (3.07 Å).

Numerous direct hydrogen bonds radiated from the boronate core and the phenolic/carboxylate moieties of the ligand.  Both boronate oxygens contacted Ser70; OB1 accepted hydrogen bonds from the Ser70 backbone amide (2.80 Å) and donated to Ser70 Oγ (2.39 Å), while OB2 engaged the same backbone amide (2.83 Å) and the side-chain Oγ (2.32 Å).  OB2 additionally donated to Glu166 Oε1 (2.87 Å) and Asn170 Oδ1 (2.76 Å).  The adjacent carbonyl O5 of the acetamide accepted a hydrogen bond from Ser130 Oγ (2.73 Å).  On the opposite face of the ligand, the phenolic O2 donated to Asn132 Nδ2 (2.86 Å) and to Asn170 Nδ2 (3.39 Å).  The carboxylate group formed a bifurcated interaction with Arg243: O4 accepted a hydrogen bond from Arg243 Nη1 (2.76 Å) and, via water 510 (vide infra), interacted with Arg243 Nη2.  The second carboxylate oxygen O3 accepted hydrogen bonds from Gly236 N (3.36 Å), Ser235 Oγ (2.76 Å), and contacted the backbone carbonyls of Ser235 (3.25 Å) and Ala237 (2.93 Å).  At the boronate nitrogen, the ligand donated to the Ala237 backbone carbonyl (3.17 Å).  Electrostatic contacts complemented these polar links: salt bridges were observed between Lys234 Nζ and O3 (3.51 Å), between Arg243 Nη1/Nη2 and O4 (2.76/3.65 Å), and between Lys73 Nζ and OB2 (3.93 Å).  Although Glu166 carries a negative charge, its Oε1 lay 2.87 Å from the partially protonated OB2, providing an additional electrostatic component to the adduct.

Hydrophobic and van der Waals contacts enclosed the inhibitor.  The phenyl ring was flanked by Tyr105, whose aromatic ring lay in an offset π-stacking geometry at 4.26 Å, and by Val216 (closest approach 4.38 Å).  Additional non-polar contacts involved Ala237 (4.30 Å), Gly236 (3.99 Å), Gly238 (4.31 Å), and the side chains of Ser70, Ser130, Ser235, Asn132, and Asn170 (closest approaches 3.62–4.12 Å).  Four ordered water molecules were found within 4 Å and six within 6 Å of the ligand.  Water 510 bridged ligand O4 to Arg243 Nη2 (ligand–water 2.46 Å, water–protein 2.86 Å) and to the backbone carbonyl of Val216 (2.53 Å), while water 533 linked the phenolic O2 to Glu104 Oε1 (2.54/2.67 Å).  Two additional waters (HOH 574 and HOH 644) were solvent-exposed and made no direct protein contacts.  No conserved catalytic waters were displaced, indicating that the entropic cost of water release upon binding was minimal in this structure.

Several protein–protein hydrogen bonds that line the active-site cleft were maintained in the ligand complex and lie within 4 Å of the inhibitor.  Ser130 Oγ hydrogen-bonded to Ser70 Oγ (3.02 Å) and to Lys73 Nζ (3.39 Å), effectively coupling the β-lactam sensor loop to the catalytic serine; Ser70 Oγ also contacted Lys73 Nζ directly (2.86 Å).  The carboxylate of Glu166 formed a hydrogen bond with Asn132 Oδ1 (3.08 Å) and a salt bridge with Lys73 Nζ (3.57 Å), interactions frequently associated with proton shuttling during catalysis.  Within the Ω-loop, Ser235 Oγ hydrogen-bonded to Gly236 N (3.48 Å) and to Lys234 Nζ (3.10 Å).  These intraprotein contacts, all within 3.5 Å of the inhibitor, framed the binding pocket and were unperturbed by compound 2, suggesting that the covalent adduct was accommodated without major rearrangement of the surrounding catalytic residues.
