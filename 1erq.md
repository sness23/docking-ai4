A covalent tetrahedral adduct was observed between compound 2f and the catalytic Ser70.  The boron atom of the inhibitor was linked to the Oγ of Ser70 at 1.45 Å.  One boronate oxygen (OB1) occupied the oxyanion hole and was hydrogen-bonded to the backbone amide nitrogens of Ser70 (2.80 Å) and Ala237 (3.07 Å).

The complex was further stabilized by an extensive hydrogen-bond network.  OB2 accepted hydrogen bonds from the Ser70 backbone amide (2.83 Å) and the Asn170 side-chain Oδ1 (2.76 Å), and donated one to Glu166 OE1 (2.87 Å).  The phenolic O5 contacted Ser70 Oγ (2.30 Å) and Ser130 Oγ (2.73 Å).  The ligand carboxylate oxygen O4 formed a bifurcated hydrogen bond with Arg243 NH1 (2.76 Å) and NH2 (3.65 Å) and interacted with Ser235 Oγ (3.32 Å).  The second carboxylate oxygen O3 hydrogen-bonded to Ser235 Oγ (2.76 Å), the backbone amide of Gly236 (3.36 Å), the backbone carbonyl of Ser235 (3.25 Å), and Ser130 Oγ (3.43 Å); it lay 3.51 Å from Lys234 NZ.  The acetamido nitrogen N1 contacted the Ala237 backbone carbonyl (3.17 Å).  The phenolic O2 accepted hydrogen bonds from Asn132 ND2 (2.86 Å) and Asn170 ND2 (3.39 Å).

Several electrostatic contacts were present.  The negatively charged ligand carboxylate formed salt bridges with Lys234 NZ (3.51 Å) and with both guanidinium nitrogens of Arg243, the closest being the NH1–O4 pair (2.76 Å).  OB2 lay within salt-bridge distance of Glu166 OE1 (2.87 Å) and approached Lys73 NZ at 3.93 Å.  Water-mediated interactions supplemented these direct contacts: water 510 bridged ligand O4 to Arg243 NH2 (ligand–water 2.46 Å, water–protein 2.86 Å) and to the Val216 backbone carbonyl (2.53 Å), while water 533 linked O2 to Glu104 OE1 (2.54 Å and 2.67 Å, respectively).

Hydrophobic and van der Waals contacts surrounded the ligand.  Side chains or main-chain atoms of Ser70, Ser130, Ser235, Ala237, Gly236, Gly238, Asn132, Asn170, Val216, and Tyr105 approached the ligand within 3.16–4.38 Å; the closest contact involved Tyr105 (3.16 Å).  The aromatic ring of compound 2f lay parallel to the Tyr105 phenol, producing a π–π stacking interaction at 4.77 Å that capped the hydrophobic face of the active site.
