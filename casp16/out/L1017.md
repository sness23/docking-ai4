No covalent linkage was observed between the ligand and the protein. In the region corresponding to the oxyanion hole, only the backbone amide nitrogen of Gly201 formed a hydrogen bond to the ligand carboxylate oxygen O21 (2.95 Å). No backbone amide of Ser203 was within hydrogen‐bonding distance of any ligand atom. 

Multiple direct hydrogen bonds were formed between the ligand and side chains in the S1 pocket. The side‐chain thiol of Cys51 donated a hydrogen bond to ligand O14 (3.47 Å). The side‐chain ammonium of Lys49 formed both a hydrogen bond and a salt bridge to ligand O15 (3.19 Å). His66 engaged ligand O15 via its backbone carbonyl oxygen (3.46 Å) and ligand O14 via its side‐chain NE2 (3.08 Å), with an additional salt‐bridge‐type interaction to ligand N1 at NE2 (3.52 Å). The catalytic Ser203 side chain donated three hydrogen bonds to the ligand: OG to O14 (3.24 Å), OG to N1 (3.33 Å), and OG to O21 (2.76 Å).

A hydrophobic shell enveloped the ligand, with van der Waals contacts to Gly220 (closest approach 3.42 Å), His66 (3.63 Å), Leu107 (3.73 Å), Lys200 (3.84 Å), Phe199 (3.76 Å), Ala228 (3.91 Å) and Ala198 (4.04 Å), among others. Aromatic π‐stacking interactions were observed in two geometries: a T‐shaped edge‐to‐face interaction with Phe199 (5.39 Å) and a parallel offset stacking with the His66 imidazole (4.02 Å).

No ordered water molecules or water‐mediated ligand contacts were present within 6 Å of the ligand. A network of protein–protein hydrogen bonds was maintained in the active site near the ligand: Ser203 OG to His66 NE2 (2.74 Å), Lys49 NZ to His66 O (2.95 Å), Cys51 SG to Phe50 O (3.45 Å), and Phe199 O to Gly201 N (3.35 Å), each with both atoms located within 4 Å of the ligand. No other inter‐protein salt bridges were observed in proximity to the inhibitor.
