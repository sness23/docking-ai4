No covalent linkages were observed between ligand L0R and the protein, and no oxyanion‐hole interactions were detected. The inhibitor formed multiple direct hydrogen bonds with side‐chain atoms in the active site. In particular, ligand atom N26 donated hydrogen bonds to Asp284 OD1 (3.13 Å) and OD2 (3.24 Å), and ligand atom O27 accepted hydrogen bonds from Asp284 OD1 (2.78 Å) and OD2 (2.91 Å). Additional hydrogen bonds included those between L0R O27 and Asp144 OD1 (3.26 Å), between O27 and Thr182 OG1 (2.73 Å), between O8 and the backbone N atom of Trp248 (3.22 Å), and between N26 and the NE2 atoms of His288 (3.24 Å) and His447 (3.25 Å).

Salt‐bridge interactions complemented the hydrogen‐bonding network. Ligand N26 engaged in salt bridges with Asp284 OD1 (3.13 Å) and OD2 (3.24 Å) and with the imidazole NE2 atoms of His288 (3.24 Å) and His447 (3.25 Å). Ligand O27 formed salt bridges to Asp284 OD1 (2.78 Å), Asp284 OD2 (2.91 Å), and Asp144 OD1 (3.26 Å), and a longer‐range electrostatic contact to His447 NE2 (3.91 Å). A predominantly hydrophobic pocket enveloped the ligand, with side chains of Phe247, Phe183, Leu186, Leu189, Tyr279 and additional aliphatic and aromatic residues providing van der Waals contacts at distances of 3.6–4.3 Å. Aromatic stacking interactions were observed between the ligand aromatic ring and Tyr279 (parallel, 4.02 Å), and T‐shaped contacts with Phe247 (4.93 Å), His288 (5.19 Å), and His447 (5.37 Å).

A network of protein–protein interactions adjacent to the binding site was also apparent. Thr182 OG1 formed hydrogen bonds with Asp144 OD2 (3.38 Å) and OD1 (3.22 Å). His288 NE2 formed a combined salt bridge and hydrogen bond to Asp284 OD1 (3.34 Å) and OD2 (3.25 Å), and Asp284 OD2 formed a salt bridge to His447 NE2 (3.14 Å) and to Asp144 OD1 (3.48 Å). The phenolic OH of Tyr279 hydrogen bonded to Asp144 OD2 (2.26 Å), and His288 NE2 and His447 NE2 formed a salt bridge at 3.19 Å. These inter‐residue contacts lie within 2.7–3.9 Å of the ligand and support the observed active‐site geometry.
