No covalent bond was formed between the ligand and the enzyme. No ligand atom was located within hydrogen‐bonding distance of the backbone amide nitrogens that constitute the oxyanion hole, and no direct hydrogen bonds between the ligand and protein residues were observed.

A single electrostatic interaction was observed between the ligand atom 5921 N1 and the side‐chain NE2 atom of His66 (3.73 Å). The ligand was otherwise encompassed by van der Waals contacts with a largely hydrophobic shell formed by Ala198 (4.28 Å), Arg221 (3.94 Å), Gly220 (3.38 Å), His66 (3.56 Å), Leu107 (3.82 Å), Lys200 (4.00 Å), Phe199 (3.49 Å), Ser203 (4.28 Å), Ser218 (4.08 Å), Ser222 (4.40 Å), Tyr219 (3.88 Å), and Val217 (3.81 Å). Aromatic π‐stacking interactions were present between the ligand heterocycle and the imidazole ring of His66 in a parallel orientation (4.33 Å), and with the phenyl ring of Phe199 in a T‐shaped geometry (5.29 Å).

No water‐mediated hydrogen bonds were detected, and no ordered water molecules were found within 6 Å of the ligand. An intramolecular network of hydrogen bonds and salt bridges among active‐site residues was maintained in the vicinity of the bound inhibitor. The main‐chain N atom of Ser203 formed a hydrogen bond to His66 NE2 (2.67 Å). His66 ND1 engaged in a salt‐bridge/hydrogen bond interaction with Asp110 OD2 (2.79 Å) and OD1 (3.42 Å). The backbone N of Arg221 hydrogen bonded to the backbone O of Gly220 (2.27 Å), and the backbone O of Phe199 contacted the side‐chain Nζ of Lys200 (2.26 Å).
