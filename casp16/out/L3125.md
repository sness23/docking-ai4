No covalent linkage was observed between L0R and the protein. Direct hydrogen bonds were formed by the ligand’s amine and carbonyl groups: the ligand N19 atom donated hydrogen bonds to the side‐chain carboxylate of Asp284 OD1 (3.03 Å) and OD2 (3.39 Å), to the imidazole NE2 of His288 (3.22 Å), to the imidazole NE2 of His447 (3.23 Å) and to the side‐chain hydroxyl of Thr182 OG1 (3.46 Å). In addition, the main‐chain amide nitrogen of Trp248 donated a hydrogen bond to the ligand O24 atom (3.27 Å).

Electrostatic interactions accompanied these hydrogen bonds. The protonated ligand amine at N19 engaged in salt bridges with the negatively charged Asp284 OD1 (3.03 Å) and OD2 (3.39 Å) and with the positively polarized imidazole NE2 atoms of His288 (3.22 Å) and His447 (3.23 Å). A second ligand amine, N20, also participated in salt bridges to His288 NE2 (3.32 Å) and His447 NE2 (3.68 Å).

The ligand was enveloped by a hydrophobic shell formed by residues lining the pocket. Van der Waals contacts were contributed by Leu186 (closest 3.61 Å), Phe247 (3.53 Å), Phe183 (3.64 Å), Thr182 (3.77 Å), Tyr187 (3.78 Å), Ala277 (3.91 Å), Trp227 (3.87 Å) and Ile140 (4.18 Å), among others. π‐Stacking interactions were observed in T-shaped geometry between the ligand aromatic moiety and the side chains of His288 (4.89 Å), Phe247 (4.67 Å) and His447 (5.26 Å).

No water‐mediated bridges were observed, and no ordered waters were modeled within 6 Å of the ligand. A network of protein–protein interactions was present near the binding site. Notable were the salt bridge between His288 NE2 and Asp284 OD1 (3.52 Å) and the combination salt bridge/hydrogen bond between Asp284 OD2 and His447 NE2 (2.88 Å), both within 3.4 Å of the ligand, as well as hydrogen bonds linking Ser142 OG to Phe278 O (3.06 Å), Tyr279 OH to Asp144 OD2 (2.43 Å) and Trp248 N to Phe247 O (2.26 Å).
