# Comparative Analysis of Three Boronic Acid Inhibitor Complexes with TEM-1 Beta-Lactamase

## The Three Compounds

All three structures contain transition state analogue boronic acid inhibitors covalently bound to the catalytic Ser70 of TEM-1 beta-lactamase. They share a common scaffold — an (R)-1-amido-2-(carboxyphenyl)ethyl boronic acid — but differ in two positions:

| PDB  | Compound | Ligand ID | Amide substituent | Phenyl ring substituent |
|------|----------|-----------|-------------------|------------------------|
| 1ERO | Compound 1 | BJP | Phenylacetamido (larger) | None |
| 1ERM | Compound 2 | BJI | Acetamido (smaller) | None |
| 1ERQ | Compound 2f | BJH | Acetamido (smaller) | 2-Hydroxy (added OH) |

Compound 1 has a bulkier phenylacetamido group. Compound 2 simplifies this to an acetamido. Compound 2f is identical to compound 2 but adds a hydroxyl group ortho to the carboxylate on the phenyl ring.

---

## Covalent Bond to Ser70

All three inhibitors form a covalent tetrahedral boronate adduct with the Oγ of Ser70, mimicking the deacylation transition state of the beta-lactam substrate.

| Structure | B–OG Distance (Å) |
|-----------|--------------------|
| 1ERM (compound 2)  | 1.39 |
| 1ERO (compound 1)  | 1.46 |
| 1ERQ (compound 2f) | 1.45 |

Compound 2 (1ERM) has the shortest covalent bond at 1.39 Å, while compounds 1 and 2f are slightly longer at 1.46 and 1.45 Å. All values are consistent with a B–O covalent linkage. The shorter bond in 1ERM may reflect the higher occupancy and tighter overall fit of the smaller acetamido compound in the active site.

---

## Oxyanion Hole

In all three structures, one boronate oxygen (OB1) is positioned in the oxyanion hole formed by the backbone amide nitrogens of Ser70 and Ala237. This is the hallmark of transition state analogue binding in class A beta-lactamases.

| Structure | OB1–Ser70 N (Å) | OB1–Ala237 N (Å) |
|-----------|------------------|-------------------|
| 1ERM | 2.73 | 3.01 |
| 1ERO | 2.85 | 3.42 |
| 1ERQ | 2.80 | 3.07 |

Compound 2 (1ERM) shows the tightest oxyanion hole geometry, with both distances shorter than the other two structures. Compound 1 (1ERO) has the longest Ala237 N distance at 3.42 Å, which is at the upper limit for a conventional hydrogen bond. This looser fit in compound 1 may be a consequence of the bulkier phenylacetamido group causing slight displacement of the boronate moiety.

## Hydrogen Bonding Network

### Conserved interactions present in all three structures

The following hydrogen bonds are observed in all three complexes and represent the core recognition elements of the active site:

**Boronate oxygens (OB1, OB2) to the catalytic machinery:**
- Ser70 backbone N to OB1 and OB2 — present in all three, distances 2.73–2.93 Å
- Ser70 Oγ to OB1 and OB2 — present in all three, distances 2.30–2.47 Å
- Ala237 backbone to OB1 — present in all three, distances 2.72–3.42 Å
- Asn170 Oδ1 to OB2 — present in all three (1ERM: 2.72 Å, 1ERO: 2.56 Å, 1ERQ: 2.76 Å)
- Glu166 OE1 to OB2 — present in all three (1ERM: 2.46 Å, 1ERO: 2.57 Å, 1ERQ: 2.87 Å)

**Carboxylate recognition:**
- Arg243 NH1 to carboxylate O4 — all three (1ERM: 2.81 Å, 1ERO: 3.12 Å, 1ERQ: 2.76 Å)
- Ser235 Oγ to carboxylate oxygens — all three, multiple contacts
- Lys234 NZ to O3 — all three (1ERM: 3.42 Å, 1ERO: 3.54 Å, 1ERQ: 3.51 Å)

**Amide nitrogen recognition:**
- N1 to Ala237 backbone carbonyl — all three (1ERM: 3.29 Å, 1ERO: 3.49 Å, 1ERQ: 3.17 Å)

**Asn132 recognition of carbonyl/phenyl substituent:**
- Asn132 ND2 to a ligand oxygen — all three (1ERM: 2.85 Å to O2; 1ERO: 2.54 Å to O6; 1ERQ: 2.86 Å to O2)

### Differences in hydrogen bonding

**Glu166 interaction strength varies:**

The Glu166 side chain forms a bidentate (bifurcated) hydrogen bond / salt bridge to OB2 in all three structures, but the geometry differs. In 1ERM, this is the shortest and tightest contact (OE1 at 2.46 Å, OE2 at 3.25 Å). In 1ERO the distances are 2.57 and 3.37 Å. In 1ERQ, only a single OE1 contact at 2.87 Å was detected within the hydrogen bond cutoff, suggesting that the hydroxyphenyl substituent in compound 2f may subtly reposition OB2 relative to Glu166.

**Ser130 is differentially engaged:**

- In 1ERM, Ser130 Oγ forms a short hydrogen bond to O3 (2.49 Å).
- In 1ERO, Ser130 does not appear as a direct hydrogen bond partner, though it contributes hydrophobic contacts (closest approach 3.38 Å).
- In 1ERQ, Ser130 Oγ makes two hydrogen bonds — to O3 (3.43 Å) and to the unique phenolic O5 (2.73 Å). The hydroxyl group of compound 2f thus recruits Ser130 into the polar network.

**Gly236 backbone involvement:**

The backbone nitrogen of Gly236 forms a hydrogen bond to the carboxylate O3 in 1ERO (3.27 Å) and 1ERQ (3.36 Å) but not in 1ERM. This may reflect a slight repositioning of the carboxylate in compounds 1 and 2f relative to compound 2.

**Unique interactions of compound 2f (1ERQ) — the hydroxyl group:**

The hydroxyl substituent on the phenyl ring of compound 2f introduces two hydrogen bonds not present in the other structures:
- O5 to Ser70 Oγ (2.30 Å) — a very short, strong hydrogen bond
- O5 to Ser130 Oγ (2.73 Å)

These additional contacts from the designed hydroxyl group provide compound 2f with polar interactions that neither compound 1 nor compound 2 can make, and likely contribute to its enhanced binding affinity.

**Lys73 engagement (1ERQ only):**

A salt bridge between OB2 and Lys73 NZ (3.93 Å) was detected only in 1ERQ. This is a long-range electrostatic contact at the edge of the salt bridge cutoff, but it is absent in the other two structures, suggesting that the hydroxyl group repositions the boronate slightly toward Lys73.

## Salt Bridges and Electrostatic Interactions

All three structures share the same core salt bridges:

| Interaction | 1ERM | 1ERO | 1ERQ |
|-------------|------|------|------|
| Arg243 NH1 – carboxylate O4 | 2.81 Å | 3.12 Å | 2.76 Å |
| Arg243 NH2 – carboxylate O4 | 3.87 Å | 3.96 Å | 3.65 Å |
| Lys234 NZ – O3 | 3.42 Å | 3.54 Å | 3.51 Å |
| Glu166 OE1 – OB2 | 2.46 Å | 2.57 Å | 2.87 Å |
| Glu166 OE2 – OB2 | 3.25 Å | 3.37 Å | 3.73 Å |
| Lys73 NZ – OB2 | -- | -- | 3.93 Å |

The Arg243 salt bridge is shortest in compound 2f (2.76 Å) and longest in compound 1 (3.12 Å). The Glu166 salt bridge is strongest in compound 2 (1ERM, 2.46 Å) and weakest in compound 2f (1ERQ, 2.87 Å). These two interactions appear to be somewhat anticorrelated — tightening one slightly loosens the other, suggesting that the boronate cannot simultaneously optimize both contacts.

---

## Hydrophobic and van der Waals Contacts

All three inhibitors are enclosed by a similar hydrophobic shell. The residues forming the shell are largely conserved:

**Present in all three structures:** Ser70, Ser130, Asn132, Asn170, Gly236, Ser235, Ala237, Tyr105, Val216

**Differences:**
- Gly238 contributes contacts in 1ERM and 1ERQ but not 1ERO
- Glu166 contributes hydrophobic contacts in 1ERM and 1ERO but not 1ERQ

The closest overall hydrophobic approach varies:

| Structure | Closest hydrophobic contact |
|-----------|-----------------------------|
| 1ERM | Ser130 at 3.47 Å |
| 1ERO | Ser130 at 3.38 Å |
| 1ERQ | Tyr105 at 3.16 Å |

Compound 2f (1ERQ) shows notably closer packing against Tyr105 (3.16 Å, 5 contacts) compared to compound 2 (1ERM, 3.54 Å, 3 contacts). This tighter Tyr105 contact in 1ERQ, combined with the additional hydroxyl hydrogen bonds, suggests that the hydroxyl substituent positions the phenyl ring more favorably against Tyr105.

Compound 1 (1ERO) has the most extensive hydrophobic contact surface overall (5 contacts to both Ser70 and Tyr105), consistent with its larger phenylacetamido group filling more of the active site cavity.

---

## Pi-Stacking with Tyr105

All three compounds engage the aromatic ring of Tyr105 in a pi-stacking interaction:

| Structure | Centroid–centroid distance (Å) |
|-----------|-------------------------------|
| 1ERM | 4.38 |
| 1ERQ | 4.77 |
| 1ERO | 5.08 |

Compound 2 (1ERM) has the tightest stacking interaction. Compound 1 (1ERO) has the most distant at 5.08 Å, which is at the outer edge of what is typically considered pi-stacking. The bulkier phenylacetamido group in compound 1 may push the carboxyphenyl ring slightly further from Tyr105.

---

## Water-Mediated Interactions

| Structure | Water bridges |
|-----------|---------------|
| 1ERM | WAT304 bridges O4 to Arg243 NH2 (2.86 / 2.78 Å) and to Val216 O (2.86 / 2.51 Å) |
| 1ERO | None detected |
| 1ERQ | WAT510 bridges O4 to Arg243 NH2 (2.46 / 2.86 Å) and to Val216 O (2.46 / 2.53 Å); WAT533 bridges O2 to Glu104 OE1 (2.54 / 2.67 Å) |

The conserved water bridge from the carboxylate O4 to Arg243 NH2 and Val216 is present in both 1ERM and 1ERQ but absent in 1ERO. The bulkier phenylacetamido group in compound 1 may displace this ordered water molecule.

Compound 2f (1ERQ) has an additional water bridge not seen in the other structures: WAT533 connects the phenolic O2 to Glu104 OE1. This water-mediated contact to Glu104 extends the hydrogen bond network further from the active site and is made possible by the hydroxyl substituent reorienting nearby water structure.

## Summary of Key Differences

| Feature | Compound 1 (1ERO) | Compound 2 (1ERM) | Compound 2f (1ERQ) |
|---------|-------------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| Amide group | Phenylacetamido (bulky) | Acetamido (compact) | Acetamido (compact) |
| Phenyl substituent | None | None | 2-Hydroxyl |
| B–OG covalent bond | 1.46 Å | 1.39 Å (shortest) | 1.45 Å |
| Oxyanion hole fit | Loosest (Ala237 N at 3.42 Å) | Tightest | Intermediate |
| Glu166 salt bridge | 2.57 Å | 2.46 Å (strongest) | 2.87 Å (weakest) |
| Arg243 salt bridge | 3.12 Å (weakest) | 2.81 Å | 2.76 Å (strongest) |
| Tyr105 pi-stacking | 5.08 Å (weakest) | 4.38 Å (strongest) | 4.77 Å |
| Unique H-bonds | Asn132 OD1 contact (O6) | -- | O5–Ser70 Oγ (2.30 Å), O5–Ser130 Oγ (2.73 Å), Lys73 NZ salt bridge |
| Water bridges | None | 1 (WAT304) | 2 (WAT510, WAT533) |
| Ser130 direct H-bond | No | Yes (2.49 Å) | Yes (2.73 Å, 3.43 Å) |

The overall picture is that compound 2 (1ERM) achieves the tightest fit in the core catalytic machinery (shortest covalent bond, best oxyanion hole geometry, strongest Glu166 contact, and closest Tyr105 stacking). Compound 2f (1ERQ) compensates for a slightly looser core fit with additional hydrogen bonds from its hydroxyl group to Ser70 Oγ and Ser130 Oγ, plus an extra water-mediated bridge to Glu104 — these additional contacts likely explain its enhanced binding affinity relative to compound 2. Compound 1 (1ERO) shows the loosest overall fit, with the longest oxyanion hole distances, weakest Arg243 salt bridge, and absence of ordered bridging waters, consistent with the bulkier phenylacetamido group causing steric strain in the binding pocket.
