The ligand was covalently linked to the enzyme through a tetrahedral boronate adduct in which the boron atom (BJI500 B) formed a single bond to the Oγ of Ser70 (1.39 Å). This attachment positioned one boronate oxygen (OB1) in the classical oxyanion hole created by the backbone amide nitrogens of Ser70 and Ala237, which were located 2.73 Å and 3.01 Å, respectively, from OB1.

Within the catalytic cleft, an extensive network of direct hydrogen bonds was observed. OB1 of the boronate accepted hydrogen bonds from both the Ser70 backbone amide (2.73 Å) and the Ala237 backbone amide (3.01 Å), while it donated a hydrogen bond to the Ser70 Oγ (2.47 Å). The second boronate oxygen (OB2) engaged Ser70 in an analogous manner (2.44 Å to Oγ and 2.83 Å to the backbone N) and accepted a short hydrogen bond from Asn170 Oδ1 (2.72 Å). Glu166 contributed a bidentate contact, with OE1 only 2.46 Å from OB2 and OE2 at 3.25 Å, constituting a charge-assisted hydrogen-bonding/salt-bridge interaction. Additional polar contacts included Asn132 ND2 to the ligand carbonyl oxygen O2 (2.85 Å) and a longer contact from Asn170 ND2 to the same oxygen (3.46 Å). The ligand amide nitrogen N1 donated hydrogen bonds to the Ala237 backbone carbonyl (3.29 Å) and the Asn170 side-chain ND2 (3.39 Å).

Peripheral to the catalytic serine, the carboxylate group of the ligand made several charge-assisted interactions. Arg243 NH1 lay 2.81 Å from O4, and Lys234 NZ approached the neighboring oxygen O3 at 3.42 Å, forming salt bridges that anchored the carboxylate in the Ω-loop region. Ser130 Oγ formed a short hydrogen bond to O3 (2.49 Å), whereas the Ser235 side chain interacted with both O3 (2.85 Å) and O4 (2.83 Å); the Ser235 backbone carbonyl contacted O3 at 3.35 Å. A water molecule (HOH 304) bridged O4 to Arg243 NH2 (ligand–water 2.86 Å, water–protein 2.78 Å) and simultaneously linked to the backbone carbonyl of Val216 (2.51 Å), extending the hydrogen-bond network toward the β3 strand.

Hydrophobic and van der Waals contacts enclosed the ligand on three sides. The boronate moiety was backed by the wall formed by Ala237, Gly236, Gly238, and Ser70, with closest C…C approaches of 3.6–4.0 Å. The acetamido arm lay against Ser130 and Asn132 (3.5–3.9 Å), while the phenyl ring was packed between Tyr105 (3.5 Å) and Val216 (4.3 Å). The phenyl ring also engaged in a π-stacking interaction with the aromatic ring of Tyr105; the centroid–centroid separation was 4.38 Å, consistent with an offset parallel arrangement. Together, these hydrophobic contacts completed the enclosure of compound 2 in the TEM-1 active site.
