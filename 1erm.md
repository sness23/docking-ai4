Compound 3 was covalently linked to the enzyme through the formation of a tetrahedral boronate adduct.  The boron atom (BJI500 B) was bonded to the Oγ of Ser70 at a distance of 1.39 Å, consistent with the canonical acyl-enzyme mimic observed for boronic acid inhibitors of class A β-lactamases.  The resulting boronate oxygen OB1 was accommodated in the classical oxyanion hole, receiving backbone-amide hydrogen bonds from Ser70 N (2.73 Å) and Ala237 N (3.01 Å).

Beyond the oxyanion hole, the ligand engaged the protein through an extensive network of direct hydrogen bonds.  The acetamide nitrogen (N1) hydrogen-bonded to the backbone carbonyl of Ala237 (3.29 Å) and to the side-chain ND2 of Asn170 (3.39 Å).  Boronate oxygen OB1 donated/accepted additional contacts to the backbone carbonyl (2.72 Å) and amide (3.01 Å) of Ala237, and to the Ser70 N and Oγ atoms (2.73 Å and 2.47 Å, respectively).  The second boronate oxygen, OB2, formed hydrogen bonds with Ser70 N (2.83 Å), Ser70 Oγ (2.44 Å), Asn170 OD1 (2.72 Å), Asn170 ND2 (3.41 Å), and both carboxylate oxygens of Glu166 (2.46 Å and 3.25 Å).  The carboxylate of the 3-carboxyphenyl ring contributed further polar contacts: O3 interacted with the side-chain Oγ of Ser130 (2.49 Å), with Ser235 O (3.35 Å) and Oγ (2.85 Å), and with Lys234 NZ (3.42 Å); O4 accepted hydrogen bonds from Ser235 Oγ (2.83 Å) and Arg243 NH1 (2.81 Å).  These latter interactions also constituted salt bridges: a cation–anion pair between Lys234 NZ and O3 (3.42 Å) and between the guanidinium of Arg243 (NH1 2.81 Å, NH2 3.87 Å) and O4.  Electrostatic contacts of opposite sign were observed between the negatively charged Glu166 carboxylate (OE1 2.46 Å, OE2 3.25 Å) and the partially negative boronate oxygen OB2.

The ligand was enveloped by a hydrophobic shell comprising Ser70, Ser130, Tyr105, Val216, Asn132, Asn170, Glu166, Ser235, Gly236, Ala237, and Gly238, with closest carbon–carbon approaches of 3.5–4.3 Å.  The aromatic phenyl ring of the inhibitor lay parallel to the Tyr105 phenol, giving a π–π stacking arrangement at 4.7 Å.  Five ordered water molecules were located within 4 Å of the ligand and a sixth within 6 Å.  Water 304 bridged between ligand atom O4 and Arg243 NH2 (2.86 Å/2.78 Å) and simultaneously contacted the backbone carbonyl of Val216 (2.51 Å), whereas water 341 formed a single hydrogen bond to ligand O2 (2.79 Å).  Waters 438, 482, and 300 occupied proximal cavities without direct ligand contacts; notably, water 300 maintained a hydrogen-bond network with Lys234 NZ and Ser235 N/Oγ, indicating that several conserved active-site waters were retained rather than displaced upon binding.

Several protein–protein hydrogen bonds bordering the ligand were preserved in the complex.  Glu166 OE1 hydrogen-bonded to Ser70 Oγ (3.49 Å) and to Lys73 NZ (3.18 Å), while Lys73 NZ in turn contacted Ser70 Oγ (2.77 Å).  Additional contacts included Ala237 O to Asn170 OD1 (3.44 Å), Lys234 NZ to Ser130 Oγ (2.70 Å) and to Ser235 O (3.07 Å), and Glu166 OE2 to Asn170 ND2 (2.79 Å).  These inter-residue interactions, all situated within 4 Å of the inhibitor, framed the ligand and completed the hydrogen-bonding network observed in the crystalline adduct.
